He is the first developer of Chrono::Engine: he has been working on numerical methods for multibody dynamics since 1998. He leads the development of the Chrono::Engine project, dealing with API design, theorethical foundations, numerical methods, etc. He is Associate Professor at the University of Parma.

Dan Negrut

University of Wisconsin, Madison

He founded the SBEL at the the University of Wisconsin, where he is Associate Professor, and since 2006 he lead many research projects rooted on Chrono::Engine developments, with special emphasis on GPU parallel computing. He is a founder of the ProjectChrono initiative.

Senior developers

This is a list of the developers that actively contribute to the development of Chrono::Engine.

Radu Serban

University of Wisconsin, Madison

Associate Scientist at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin. Among other things, he developed the new build tool chain, and he designed parts of the API software architecture.

Hammad Mazhar

University of Wisconsin, Madison

PhD student at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin. He is expert in GPU coding, parallel computation and massive dynamics for granular simulations. Among other things, he contributed to the development of the GPU collision detection.

Justin Madsen

University of Wisconsin, Madison

PhD student at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin. His researches are about ground-vehicle interaction. He developed Chrono::Engine tools for vehicle dynamics.

Toby Heyn

University of Wisconsin, Madison

Former PhD student at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin. Among other things, he collaborated to the development of the code for the MPI version of Chrono::Engine, and developed new types of solvers for the CCP problem.

Andrew Seidl

University of Wisconsin, Madison

Graduate student at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin. He is our system administrator, he manages the servers for nightly builds, automatic documentation, regression testing, etc.

Arman Pazouki

University of Wisconsin, Madison

PhD student at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin. He contributed to the experimental Chrono tools for fluid-structure interaction.

Dan Melanz

University of Wisconsin, Madison

PhD student at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin. He contributed to the experimental Chrono code for finite element simulation of flexible structures using the ANCF formulations.

Naresh Khude

University of Wisconsin, Madison

PhD Student at the Mechanical Engineering Department of the University of Wisconsin, SBEL member. He developed experimental GPU code for ANCF finite elements.

Aaron Bartholomew

University of Wisconsin, Madison

Daniel Kaczmarek

University of Wisconsin, Madison

Undergraduate Student in Computer Sciences at the University of Wisconsin. He develops the [http://sbel.wisc.edu/Resources/Software/ChronoEngine/c_render Chrono::Render] toolchain.
